Welcome to the ISAK admin 👋

This is the control room for the website. It's deliberately simple. Here's everything you need to know.

Signing in

  1. Go to /admin.html.
  2. Sign in with the email and password you set from your invite email.
  3. Forgot your password? Use the "Forgot password?" link on the sign-in screen.

Adding an event

Open the Events tab → fill in the form → Save event.

  • Title, date, time, location — what shows on the card.
  • Image — upload a landscape photo (roughly 1200×800). It's stored in Supabase and shown on the card.
  • Published — tick this to make it visible on the website. Leave unticked to keep it a draft.
Events appear automatically on the Events page and the home page (next 3 upcoming). If there are no published upcoming events, the site shows the built-in example cards instead — so the page is never empty.

Approving members

Open the Members tab. New sign-ups arrive as pending. They already get their welcome email + WhatsApp invite automatically — "Activate" is just for your own tracking. You can export the full list as CSV any time.

The roadmap & automated reminders

The Roadmap tab powers two things:

  • The public roadmap on the About page (any milestone with "Show on public roadmap" ticked).
  • Automated email reminders. Each milestone has a due date, recipient emails and "reminder lead days". A daily job emails the recipients once the reminder window opens (e.g. 30 days before the e.V. registration deadline), then marks it sent so it never repeats.
Example already loaded: "Register ISAK as e.V." due Sep 2026 will email anchalgupta.chp@gmail.com 30 days before. Edit the recipients/dates as your team grows. Use "Reset reminder" to make it send again.

Leads & subscribers

Leads = students (often still in India) who asked for the pre-arrival guide. Subscribers = newsletter signups. Both are exportable to CSV for your mailing tool.

Inviting another admin

Open the Admins tab → enter their name + email → Send invite. They'll get an email to set a password and can then sign in.

Good habits

  • Keep content honest — we're a new association and that's our strength.
  • Use real photos from our events wherever you can.
  • Never share the admin password; invite people properly instead.
